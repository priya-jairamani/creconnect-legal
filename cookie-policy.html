About This Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how CreConnect uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit and use our Platform. It also explains your choices regarding cookies. This Policy supplements our Privacy Policy and is part of our broader commitment to transparency.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to enhance user experience, and to provide information to website owners.

In addition to cookies, we may also use similar tracking technologies such as:

  • Local Storage / Session Storage: Browser-based storage used to maintain session state and user preferences.
  • Pixels / Web Beacons: Small transparent images embedded in pages or emails to track interactions.
  • Fingerprinting: Device or browser characteristics used for security and fraud detection purposes only.

For the purposes of this Policy, we refer to all such technologies collectively as "cookies."

4. How We Use Cookies

CreConnect uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Authentication & Security: To securely log you in, maintain your session, and protect against unauthorized access and CSRF attacks.
  • Platform Functionality: To remember your preferences, settings, and onboarding progress.
  • Performance & Analytics: To understand how users navigate the Platform so we can improve features, fix issues, and optimize performance.
  • Social Media Integration: To facilitate Facebook Login and Instagram account connection via the Facebook Graph API.

We do not use cookies for:

  • Targeting you with advertising based on your browsing behavior.
  • Selling your data to third-party advertisers.
  • Tracking you across other websites for advertising purposes.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit the Platform, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner. You can choose to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customize your preferences by category. Your preferences will be saved for 12 months.

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking "Cookie Settings" in the footer of any page on the Platform.

5.2 Browser Controls

You can also control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete existing cookies.
  • Block all or specific types of cookies.
  • Set preferences for specific websites.
  • Enable "Do Not Track" signals (note: not all websites honor DNT).

5.4 Facebook Cookie Controls

You can manage Facebook's use of cookies by visiting Facebook's Ad Preferences and adjusting your settings. Note that this controls Facebook's own cookie behavior, not CreConnect's first-party cookies.

6. Cookie Duration

Cookies on the Platform fall into two duration categories:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that exist only during your current browser session. They are deleted when you close your browser. Used for authentication tokens and CSRF protection.
  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device for a specified period (shown in the cookie inventory above). Used for remembering preferences and analytics. These expire automatically or can be deleted manually via browser settings.

7. Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal that requests websites not track your browsing activity. CreConnect respects DNT signals where technically feasible. When a DNT signal is detected, we will not use analytics cookies or non-essential tracking technologies for that session.

Note: DNT signals have no effect on essential cookies required for Platform operation or on Meta's own cookies set during the Facebook Login process.

Your continued use of the Platform after the updated Policy is posted constitutes your acceptance of the changes.
